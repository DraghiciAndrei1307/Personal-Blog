About Me

This is what I do.

I’m a backend-focused engineer with a strong foundation in databases and a growing passion for building reliable, scalable systems.

My journey started with database administration, where I developed an appreciation for performance, data integrity, and the systems that power real-world applications. Over time, I naturally transitioned toward software development and automation, working with Python to build and improve tools, services, and workflows.

I enjoy turning ideas into working products — from web applications to automation scripts — and I’m especially interested in the intersection of backend development and DevOps. Writing clean code, structuring projects properly, and making things run consistently (locally or in containers) are things I care about.

Lately, I’ve been focusing on strengthening my engineering practices: testing, CI/CD, and containerization. I believe that good software isn’t just about making it work, but making it maintainable, predictable, and easy to evolve.
